BACKGROUND
Brightspeed is the nation's fifth largest ILEC, valued at over 7.5 billion USD, with a service territory encompassing more than 6.5 million locations in mainly rural and suburban communities. The company has officially commenced the buildout of its state-of-the-art fiber optics network with plans to complete over 1 million new fiber passings by the end of 2023. Brightspeed is a spin-off of Lumen and CenturyLink's current rural business and consumer market. Brightspeed services the southeast, southwest, and great lakes regions of the United States. Brightspeed is a fiber-first telecom brand that sells B2C and B2B voice and internet solutions. It is a new business spun-off from parts of Lumen and CenturyLink.
THE PROBLEM
In November 2021, Apollo Global Management (A hedgefund) struck a deal with Lumen Technologies to acquire Brightspeed business in rural areas across the United States. HCx was introduced to the Brightspeed client relationship as of March 2022 to help with migration of "cloned pages" from Lumen (formally CenturyLink) to Brightspeed.com Business experience.
Currently, in addition to the above effort we are also involved in the design of new Enterprise & wholesale landing pages and product pages for Brightspeed.com. There is also a small team of CGI developers who are developing said pages in Adobe Experience Manager (AEM).
The org's focus is on increasing customer acquisition while reducing customer attrition through improved CX with the launch of new products.
MY ROLE & ROADMAP
I joined the team as senior product designer alongside a UX designer, UX writer and lead UX Strategist for the whole migration effort. My team was brought on to help the new transition from Lumen (formally CenturyLink) to Brightspeed. We immediately had two main goals that would span close to a years worth of work.
  • Help with the migration of "cloned pages" from Lumen.com and Centurylink.com to the new Brightspeed.com Enterprise & wholesale digital experience and launch by October 2022.
  • Build a new 2.0 website experience for Brightspeed and launch mid January 2023.

RESULTS
BRIGHTSPEED BUSINESS LANDING PAGE
We first built the Business Solutions page using components that we built based on the requirements we gathered from our competitive analysis, proto personas, and our clients at Brightspeed. This pages purpose is to help users navigate to their business segment (Enterprise, Wholesale, Public Sector, Small Business, or Channel Partners.) We wanted to highlight different areas of support and sales on the page, and touch on subjects like upcoming events, promotions, and of course content to support the brand.
We weren't able to modify the branding of few components like the 'Contact form' since it was connected to the Marketo form from the Lumen website experience and due to tight deadlines, we coudln't afford to develop a new component for it. The first built components included:
  • A hero header / carousel
  • A link bar
  • Image + text component
  • Card boxes with icons
  • Contact bar (Sales & support)
