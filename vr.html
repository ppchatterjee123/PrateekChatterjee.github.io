BACKGROUND
As a part of my Master's Thesis, I created a car-driving game for evaluating the user experience of new drivers to help them learn driving rules and regulations of road.
The game allows the player to experience an immersive environment in Virtual reality. The goal of the game is to allow players to give a road test, which will help them to learn rules and regulations of driving and alert them about the types of mistakes they are making via audio and visual feedback. Also, it will determine if the player passed or failed the test. I have conducted A/B testing to determine simulators effectiveness against the RMV Drivers Manual.
The User Interface consists of 10 tasks, visually generated alerts, visually generated feedback, speedometer and a minimap. Depending on the player's accuracy, it will notify them, if they passed or failed in the test.
The project is developed using Unity Game Engine, C#, Photoshop and Maya3D. The UX research was carried out using google docs and Microsoft excel. This project is an attempt to make users get acquainted with the rules and regulations of driving, also to make them understand the consequences of driving reckless and unsafe driving. Users can play on Google VR and Occulus VR systems. There will be more support to other VR systems in future. You do not need a VR headset to play the current uploaded game in this webpage. In case you need to try out the VR version of the game, then kindly send me a request through email.
THE PROBLEM
Reading from driver's manual makes it long, tiring, and boring to learn driving rules and regulations. Many user's fail in their first few driver's exam and have to re-appear again.
THE SOLUTION
Designed and developed an immersive VR game with realistic environment. The game is based on Audio/Visual feedback and alerts and a UI for instant scoring system, which allows the user to rectify their mistakes and learn from them effortlessly.

QUANTITATIVE RESEARCH
Did usability testing with 70 users, which gave me insights and constructive feedback regarding improvements and further requirements to make the product viable and useful.
I developed the final version of the game (few screenshots above) and conducted A/B testing with 22 users to evaluate whether the simulator is effective compared to the manual for learning rules and regulations of the road.
All these users were non-drivers. There were two groups such as the control group and the experimental group. Eleven users were in the control group, while the rest eleven users participated in the experimental group.
During the user-testing period, users from both groups were asked to appear for a learner’s driving quiz test. The quiz format was according to the actual learner’s driving test format and similar type of questions were asked.
Take the QUIZ!!!
The question above is related to HOV lane and users have to select the correct answer. Similarly, within the game, the user is alerted about HOV zone and they are not allowed to drive in that particular zone as show below:
A total of eleven people from the experimental group gave the quiz test mentioned above. After taking the test, they were asked to play the VR game. After completing the game, the users were again asked to take the same quiz.
Just like the experimental group, eleven people from the control group appeared for the quiz test as well. After appearing for the test, they were asked to read the RMV manual, which consisted of all the answers to the questions mentioned in the quiz. After reading the manual, they were asked to appear for the Quiz test again. It was found that, they were able to evaluate the correct answers to the given questions in the test.
However, it was not possible for me to include all the questions from the manual in the game and so I had to reduce the scope of the project. Therefore, I presented them with only few pages of RMV manual to keep the scope small and this allowed the users to focus on the answers in short period of time.
FINAL RESULT
Users from both the groups appeared for the first quiz test. It was seen that four users failed the test from the experimental group, while only three users failed from control group. Then I conducted user testing by letting the users from both the groups to perform their respective task, for instance, the users from experimental group were told to play the simulator, while the other group were told to read the RMV manual. After letting them to do their respective tasks, users from both the groups took the quiz test for the second time. It was seen that users performed better and passed in the test after learning from the game and manual as well.
