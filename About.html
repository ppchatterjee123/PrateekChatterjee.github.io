Job experience
I am a Senior UX Designer and Consultant with 5+ years of experience in helping clients across the federal gov., healthcare, telecom, automotive, business consulting industries.
My story
Currently, I work at CGI in the Washington DC - Virginia area. My journey began in 2015 when I obtained a Bachelor's degree in Engineering (CS) from Mumbai University in India. In 2016, I relocated to the United States and got the opportunity to pursue a Master's degree in Interactive Media & Game Development at WPI, with a specialization in HCI. During my time as a graduate student, I joined Carmaguys, a startup for an automotive car service company, where I was involved in various aspects of design and development. Upon completing my master's program, I made the decision to become a full stack designer and had the previlage to join Softrams as a UX designer.
CGI Inc. experience
In my recent role at CGI, I played a crucial part in the successful migration of over 50 webpages from Lumen and CenturyLink for a multi-billion dollar Telecom company. This involved implementing a new and redesigned business experience within an exceptionally tight deadline.
In recognition of my significant contribution to delivering a high-quality product, I was apploauded with the 'Partnership and Quality' award at CGI.
Softrams experience
During my time at Softrams, I had the privilege of taking the lead in UX research and design for two notable products. The first one is ACO-MS, a healthcare product catering to a federal agency under the United States Department of Health and Human Services. The second product is H-Drive, a Healthcare SaaS solution. My primary objective was to develop user-friendly and robust products that not only provide a pleasant experience for users but also contribute to the growth and success of the business.
According to the latest performance data for the ACO-MS product (Federal Agency Product), Accountable care organizations (ACO Users) had a record year in 2019. ACO Users saved $1.2 billion in Medicare in 2019 as compared to $739 million in 2018. (More Info)
